Builder Program

You don't need to know if your idea will work. You need a way to find out.

Builder is Opportunity Machine's ten-week program for idea-to-seed-stage founders. You'll test your idea against real customers — with a cohort of founders doing the same thing beside you.

Arrived from "I'm building, but I'm not sure I'm building the right thing"?

You've already started. That's not the problem.

Quiet traction is one of the most stressful places to be as a founder — you're not stalled enough to obviously quit, and not confirmed enough to confidently keep going.

You've put real time into this. Maybe a prototype, a landing page, a few early users. The response has been... fine. Not a clear yes. Not a clear no. Just quiet.

That's exactly what customer conversations are for — not to start over, but to find out what to keep, what to change, and what to stop. Nothing you've built is wasted. It's data. You just haven't asked it the right questions yet.

The honest version

You could keep collecting advice. You could keep polishing the deck. You could start building and hope.

Or you could spend ten weeks finding out what's actually true.

What you'll prove

What Builder helps you prove

Who your customer really is

Not the demographic. The person, the role, the day they're having.

What they'll actually pay for

From their words and their behavior — not your pitch.

Whether the business works

Pricing, costs, and a path to market that survives contact with reality.

The four questions

The method

Weekly sessions with your cohort. 15+ real customer conversations as the spine of the program. Mentor feedback along the way. A pitch night at the end — built on what you proved, not what you hoped.

What you walk out with

A pitch built on evidence, not hope

  • A customer picture built from real conversations you ran yourself — not secondhand research
  • A value proposition and MVP scope you can defend, because you tested it before you committed to it
  • A business model that's been pressure-tested against what customers actually said, not just what you hoped they'd say
  • A pitch built on evidence — and a cohort and mentor network that doesn't expire at graduation

Or — and this is a real outcome we're proud of — you leave knowing your first idea wasn't it, with your savings intact and a sharper eye for the next one. Every cohort runs this discipline the same way: ask first, build second.

Is this you?

Who it's for, and who it's probably not

Builder is for you if:

  • You have an idea — or an early build — and you're willing to test it honestly
  • You can commit to weekly sessions and real customer conversations
  • You'd rather know than hope

Builder probably isn't for you if:

  • You're looking for someone to validate a decision you've already made
  • You can't make time for customer conversations between sessions
  • You mainly need capital, not clarity — we can point you toward funding conversations, but Builder isn't a check
Questions

FAQ

Do I need a finished idea?

No. Half-formed is normal. The program exists to form it.

Do I need to be technical?

No. Several of our strongest founders aren't. Knowing your customer beats knowing how to code.

What if I find out my idea doesn't work?

Then you found out with real customer conversations instead of a finished product — and you kept the time and money that would have gone into building something nobody asked for. We consider that a win. Genuinely, not as a consolation prize.

How much time does it take?

One session a week, plus the customer conversations between them. If you can give it 4–6 focused hours a week, you can do Builder.

What happens on the interest call?

Twenty minutes with our team. You tell us where you are; we tell you honestly whether Builder fits — and if it doesn't, where to go instead. Nothing to prepare.
